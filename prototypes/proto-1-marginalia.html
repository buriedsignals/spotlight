§1 — What it does

Five moves that turn a lead into a publishable finding.

  • Takes a lead — URL, name, topic, document — and designs a methodology you approve before anything runs.[3]
  • Runs research cycles with an independent fact-checker after each one. Nothing advances on one source or one agent's reasoning.[4]
  • Every finding arrives sourced and stress-tested — primary documents anchored, two independent sources per claim, disputes flagged, affected perspectives present.[5]
  • Review page for you or your editor — click through findings, challenge claims, request deeper verification. Your feedback re-triggers the investigator on specific points.[6]
  • Builds a living knowledge base — entities, sources, tools, and methodology stay cross-linked. "We already profiled this shell company in the Darfur case" instead of starting from zero.[7]
§2 — What it integrates with

Pluggable where it matters. Opinionated where it counts.

  • Agent runtimes — local journalism fine-tune, Claude Code, Gemini, Codex, OpenCode. One set of skills, many runtimes.[8]
  • Web scraping, search, evidence archiving — every source captured locally with chain of custody.[9]
  • OSINT Navigator — 1,000+ curated OSINT tools with AI-powered lookup, surfaced live per task.[10]
  • Monitoring feeds — conflict events, disaster alerts, global news, investigative outlets.[11]
  • Browser automation, narrative tracking, misinformation databases — plug-in integrations; the framework takes more.[12]
